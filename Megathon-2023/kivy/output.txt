Great, I'm glad you're interested in learning about fever symptoms! Here are some common symptoms of fever:

1. Elevated body temperature: The most obvious symptom of fever is an elevated body temperature, usually above 98.6°F (37°C).
2. Chills: People with fever often experience chills, which can be accompanied by shaking or trembling.
3. Sweating: Fever can cause excessive sweating, which may be visible on the forehead, palms, and soles of the feet.
4. Fatigue: Feeling tired, weak, and exhausted are common symptoms of fever.
5. Headache: A headache can be a symptom of fever, especially if the fever is severe.
6. Muscle aches: Fever can cause muscle aches and pains, especially in the back, arms, and legs.
7. Joint pain: Some people with fever may experience joint pain, particularly in the knees, elbows, and wrists.
8. Sore throat: A sore throat can be a symptom of fever, especially if it's accompanied by a cough or difficulty swallowing.
9. Cough: A mild to moderate cough can be a symptom of fever, especially if it's accompanied by a sore throat.
10. Shortness of breath: In severe cases of fever, people may experience shortness of breath or difficulty breathing.
11. Confusion or disorientation: In rare cases, fever can cause confusion or disorientation, especially in older adults or people with underlying medical conditions.
12. Seizures: In rare cases, fever can cause seizures, especially in children under the age of 5.
It's important to note that not everyone will experience all of these symptoms, and the severity of the fever can vary from person to person. If you or someone you know is experiencing any of these symptoms, it's important to seek medical attention to determine the cause and receive appropriate treatment.
